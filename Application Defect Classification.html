Application Defect Classification

The process of identifying bugs or defects in IT applications is fundamental for all QA (Quality Assurance) professionals. It's their primary responsibility to ensure applications function flawlessly, catching and addressing defects before they impact users.

Recognizing and Marking Defects

     - To prevent defects from recurring, it's crucial to accurately identify them and document their specifics. This includes capturing where and how the defect manifests and analyzing its potential impact on the application and users.

Key Question: How to Achieve This?

The solution lies in two key actions:

     - Classification of Defects: Categorizing defects based on their type, severity, and affected area in the application.

     - Root Cause Analysis: Investigating and identifying the underlying reason for the defect. This step helps ensure corrective measures address the actual source of the problem, reducing the likelihood of recurrence.

Roles and Responsibilities

These tasks are collaborative efforts involving:

     - Scrum Masters: They facilitate teams to improve workflows and track progress on defect resolution.

     - QE (Quality Engineering) Specialists: They focus on broader engineering principles to optimize quality across the application lifecycle.

     - JIRA Board Owners: They manage JIRA boards, organizing defect tracking and ensuring actionable items are addressed promptly.
