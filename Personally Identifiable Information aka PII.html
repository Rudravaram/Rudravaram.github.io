PII Categories

1. Sensitive PII: Includes financial details, health records, biometric data, and IDs, where unauthorized exposure poses severe risks.

2. Non-Sensitive PII: Public details like names or addresses that can become sensitive when combined with other data.

Why Protecting PII Matters

- Prevents Identity Theft: Stops misuse of private data.

- Ensures Compliance: Aligns with laws like GDPR and CCPA.

- Builds Trust: Preserves organizational reputation by protecting sensitive data.

How PII is Protected

- Encryption: Secures data during storage and transmission.

- Access Controls: Restricts access to authorized users.

- Anonymization: Removes identifiable elements to ensure privacy.

- Audit Logs: Tracks who accesses or modifies data.

- Training: Educates staff on proper data handling practices.

In short, safeguarding PII is crucial for privacy, trust, and legal compliance in our digital age.
